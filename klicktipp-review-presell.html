KlickTipp Review: Tag-Based Email Automation for Creators and Coaches

A practical look at what KlickTipp does well, where it falls short, and whether it fits your business — before you commit to a subscription.

Quick verdict

KlickTipp is a strong pick if you already run funnels, courses, or coaching offers and want behavior-based automation instead of static email lists. It's less ideal if you just need a simple newsletter tool — in that case it may feel like overkill.

What KlickTipp actually is

KlickTipp is an email and SMS marketing automation platform built in Germany. Instead of organizing subscribers into fixed lists, it uses a tag-based system: every contact gets tagged based on what they click, buy, or do, and those tags trigger automations in real time. That means a lead who downloads a guide and later buys a product moves through your funnel automatically, without you manually shuffling them between lists.

It's built for people running funnels — course creators, coaches, agencies, and online stores — rather than for someone who just wants to send a monthly newsletter. The platform has an English interface and connects natively with US, UK, and Canadian payment tools, along with 300+ other integrations including Shopify, Stripe, and Zapier.

Who it's a good fit for

  • Course creators and coaches who need automated follow-up sequences based on subscriber behavior
  • Agencies managing multiple client funnels that need clean tagging instead of list chaos
  • E-commerce sellers who want purchase-triggered email flows
  • Anyone who needs GDPR-ready data handling built in, not bolted on

Pros and cons

Strengths

  • Tag-based system avoids duplicate contacts across lists
  • Strong deliverability track record
  • Built-in GDPR compliance tools
  • 300+ integrations, including Shopify and Stripe
  • Real human support, not just chatbots

Trade-offs

  • Steeper learning curve than basic newsletter tools
  • Tag-based logic takes time to fully understand
  • Overkill if you only send occasional newsletters
